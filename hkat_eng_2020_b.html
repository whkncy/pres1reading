Savour Hong Kong

If you are reading this, you might be planning a trip to Hong Kong. One thing not to be missed is the Hong Kong Tourism Board's annual event, Savour Hong Kong. It has attracted more than 5 million local and overseas visitors over the past 20 years, and is a 'must-go-to' winter event. This year, we aim to provide our visitors with the most extraordinary flavours from all around the world. Visit our webpage (http://savourhk.lovehk.com) for the latest news and events.

Date: 15th - 30th December, 2020
Time: 10:30 a.m. - 8:00 p.m.
Venue: Central Harbourfront Event Space
Admission Fee: $20 per person (free for children aged 3 or below and seniors aged 65 or above)

Natural Drinker (Stall: M06)

For thousands of years, herbal tea has been popular with Chinese people who dislike going to see the doctor and taking pills. Many believe that regularly drinking herbal tea can prevent them from getting sick. Although most of the teas are black, not all of them are bitter. Come and experience the magic of Chinese plants and fruit yourself!

Pearl of the Orient (Stall: F30)

Hexagoners, and people from all over the world, line up to try our local snacks. Excite your taste buds with our famous fresh pineapple buns and egg tarts. Everything is baked on-the-spot! Feeling sleepy after your long flight? Try our 'yuen yeung' a famous local drink made by mixing milk tea with coffee! You will surely feel energised!

Neo-Canton (Stall: G19)

Let our American-born Chinese chef change how you feel about dim sum. Try our famous piggy-shaped barbecue pork buns, shrimp dumplings made with tomato-flavoured wrappers and chicken feet in lemonade. We bring the best of the East and West together for you to taste. How can you say 'no' to high-quality food at low prices?

Hiss Hiss (Stall: K22)

Are you afraid of snakes? What if they are cooked? Seasonal snake soup was one of the favourite dishes of ancient Chinese emperors on cold, icy days. Nowadays, it is very famous among Hongkongers, but some still find it strange. Its main ingredients are fresh snake meat, chicken, ginger and mushrooms. You will be surprised how drinking snake soup can warm you up in minutes!

How to Be a Good Speaker

If the thought of speaking in front of the class gives you cold sweats, you are not alone. Learning certain speaking skills can help you gain confidence and make an impressive presentation. Here are some tips for you.

Paragraph (i)
Everyone knows the importance of having interesting ideas in a presentation. However, there are other essential things. According to Roy M. Berko, an expert in communication, only 7% of the meaning comes from the words used. 93% of the meaning of what people say comes from non-verbal signals, such as facial expressions and body language. How to use your voice is also important. The emotions in your voice have to match what you are saying. An appropriate tone and style should also be used during your presentation.

Paragraph (ii)
While giving a presentation, most speakers find it difficult to control their speed, especially when they are nervous. You may think that speaking rapidly like lightning would help you relax. However, it does not help you give a good presentation. When you deliver a speech in a rush, it is hard for the audience to follow, and they might get tired of listening to you. Why not slow down to let your words be heard more clearly and to be better understood?

Paragraph (iii)
You should also pay extra attention to how you break down a sentence into parts by taking pauses whenever needed. For example, when you say 'Let's eat, Grandpa', you have to pause after 'eat' so that the sentence sounds like a friendly invitation. Otherwise, people may think that you are asking others to 'eat Grandpa' with you! Pausing correctly can save you from an embarrassing situation.

Paragraph (iv)
To conclude, your non-verbal signals, tone, speed and pauses all contribute to how well your audience understands your presentation. Practise more and you will be able to give an impressive presentation confidently next time!

Carnival Fun

The world-famous ABC Carnival has come back to the New Central Harbourfront this Christmas. After waiting for a year, finally I got the chance to go there again yesterday. Since Uncle Sam from Canada has returned to Hong Kong, we decided to have some fun together at the carnival. It was a Friday afternoon and tickets were cheaper than expected. How lucky we were!

When Uncle Sam and I arrived at the carnival, our jaws dropped. We didn't expect to see so many different attractions in such a small area in Central. We didn't even know where to start. I wanted to start with the game stalls, but Uncle Sam suggested going on the rides first so that we wouldn't have to carry any big soft toys around with us.

On our way to the rides, we grabbed some hot dogs and drinks because we didn't want to wait for hours to eat in the open-air cafe. Seeing the long queues for the rides, we thought we would be bored to death. Fortunately, while waiting, some street artists were performing nearby. They were very entertaining. The rides were so exciting and Uncle Sam screamed and laughed even louder than I did. He usually looks serious but that day I couldn't believe he was such a kidult!

I've posted some selfies here. The first picture shows the delicious hot dogs. How fun it is to see my scared face on the rides in the second picture! But I like the third picture most because both Uncle Sam and I are making funny faces, and I am holding my favourite teddy bear in my arms. Uncle Sam looks particularly silly with the bunny ears on his head!

Playing stall games at the end of the day was the most unforgettable experience as Uncle Sam won five toys for me. Thanks, Uncle Sam!
